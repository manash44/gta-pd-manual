Introduction

The Los Santos Police Department is the heartbeat of law enforcement in the vibrant and dynamic city of Los Santos. As an officer of the LSPD, you are entrusted with the crucial responsibility of maintaining peace, upholding the law, and serving the community with integrity and professionalism.

Our Mission: To Protect and Serve. We strive to ensure the safety, security, and well-being of all residents and visitors of Los Santos through dedicated law enforcement and proactive community engagement.

Administrative Policies

Ride-Alongs

Civilians must submit a Statement of Interest and be screened. Only Command/HighCommand/FTO Lead can approve.

  • Civilians wear white/gray tactical vest. No PD uniforms.
  • Do not interfere, speak at incidents, or use radio.
  • Stay in vehicle unless instructed.

Departmental Transfers

Requires formal written request, review by current Head, and approval by receiving Department Head.

Disciplinary Actions

  • 1st Action: Warning
  • 2nd Action: LEO /13 (Repeated infractions)
  • 3rd Action: Summons + 12-24 Hour Suspension
  • 4th Action: 3/3 Strike + Termination

Traffic Stop Procedures

Standard Stop

  1. Initiate stop with lights/sirens. Notify Dispatch of location and vehicle.
  2. Position vehicle 1-2 car lengths behind, angled 45 degrees.
  3. Approach with caution. Touch trunk.
  4. Identify self, request License/Registration/Insurance.
  5. Run checks. Issue citation or warning.
  6. Clear stop: "Dispatch, show my traffic Code 4."

Felony Stop

For stolen vehicles or warrants. High risk.

  1. Wait for backup if possible.
  2. Hold suspect at gunpoint. Commands: "Engine off, keys out window".
  3. Order driver out, backwards, hands up. Secure driver first.
  4. Secure passengers one by one.
  5. Clear vehicle trunk.

Use of Force Continuum

Level 1: Officer Presence Visual deterrence, professional attitude.
Level 2: Verbalization Calm commands, increasing volume if needed.
Level 3: Empty Hand Control Soft (holds) and Hard (strikes) techniques.
Level 4: Less-Lethal Baton, Pepper Spray, Taser.
Level 5: Lethal Force Firearm. Defense of life only.

Vehicle Pursuits

Primary Unit: Focuses on driving. Updates on hazards.

Secondary Unit: Handles Comms. Updates location/speed/conditions.

Tertiary Unit: Proactive maneuvers (Boxing, etc).

Intervention Tactics

  • PIT: Requires Senior LEO approval. Not on motorcycles.
  • Spikes: Preemptive or during pursuit. Requires Senior LEO approval.
  • Lethal: Last resort. Requires authorization.

Gang War Situations

City Situation

  • Establish perimeter and evacuate civilians.
  • Repeated warnings to cease conflict.
  • Intervene if conflict continues.
  • If agreement to leave: Allow exit, but bodies/evidence remain.

Outside City (Sandy/Paleto)

  • Establish perimeter and gather evidence (Air-1/Cameras).
  • Non-Interference Policy: Monitor only. Do not engage.
  • Post-Conflict: Process scene, treat injuries, collect evidence.

EMS Interactions

  • Ensure scene is Code 4 before EMS entry.
  • Do not rush EMS. They determine transport needs.
  • Suspects must be searched prior to transport.
  • LEO escort required for suspect/LEO transport.

Negotiation & Breaching

Negotiation

Identify suspects/hostages and demands. Never exchange: Police equipment, vehicles, or prisoners.

Priority is safety of all individuals.

Breaching

Authorized when: Negotiations fail, lives at stake, or verbal contact lost.

1 Assign teams (Left, Right, Long).
2 Call: "Callsign Ready" -> "Set" -> "BREACH".
3 Button Hook Entry: Stack on opposite sides, clearer enters their side/blind spots.

Custody Operations

Booking

  1. Place in cell. Remove restraints only when locked.
  2. Secondary search. Remove contraband.
  3. Fingerprint & Mugshot if needed (Waist up, no face coverings).
  4. Draft charges. Read Miranda if not done.
  5. Right to Attorney: Attempt contact.

Transport

Notify DOC on Radio Channel 5 with Prisoner Name, Description, Time.

If DOC unavailable: Transport to Bolingbroke (Bus/Van) with escort.

Evidence & Warrants

Evidence Collection

  • Prevent contamination (Gloves).
  • Photograph in place.
  • Collect sample of all evidence.
  • Submit: 1.#### (Arrest), 2.#### (Civilian), 3.#### (Investigation).

Search Warrants

Requires Probable Cause & Supervisor Approval. Valid for 30 days. Only seize listed items.

Arrest Warrants

  • Must be written immediately with existing evidence.
  • Validity: Must be created within 3 days of incident.
  • Includes: Charges, fine, time, and issuing LEO.
  • Void if not served during booking.

BOLO (Be On The Lookout)

Not an arrest warrant. Used for questioning/investigation awareness.

Include vehicle plate or ID. Update associated reports when resolved.
