Miranda Rights

Rights During Custodial Interrogation

Miranda rights are a set of rights that law enforcement must inform individuals of before conducting a custodial interrogation. These rights are derived from the Fifth Amendment's protection against self-incrimination and the Sixth Amendment's right to counsel.

Clear Communication

These rights must be clearly conveyed to the individual, and they must affirmatively waive these rights to have their statements used in court.

Right to Stop Interrogation

If a person in custody indicates they wish to remain silent or request an attorney, the interrogation must stop immediately.

Timing of Miranda Warnings

Miranda rights are not always read during an arrest but are required before a custodial interrogation begins.

Jurisdictional Variations

The specific wording can vary between jurisdictions, but the key elements must be adequately conveyed.
