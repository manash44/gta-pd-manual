Penal Codes

Document Maintained by Deputy Chief Ray Holt

Jail time is capped at 80 minutes without trial. With Trial Uncapped.

Minutes equals months in RP

Capital Punishment (death penalty) = Judge ruling is the only way to recieve the death penalty.

Methods include: Firing Line, Life in prison

Class Charge Maximum Punishment Definition Aggravating Circumstances
1 MURDER MAXIMUM SENTENCE DEFINITION AGGRAVATING CIRCUMSTANCES
a First Degree Murder 40 minutes (+10 minutes per aggravated circumstance) Directly or indirectly causing the death of a person (not revived by EMS) with pre-meditated intent. Aggravated circumstances: (i) The victim was an on-duty LEO or a government official.
b Second Degree Murder 35 minutes (+10 minutes per aggravated circumstance) Directly or indirectly causing the death of a person (not revived by EMS) that is either unplanned and intentional or caused by wreckless disregard to human life. Aggravated circumstances: (i) The victim was an on-duty LEO or a government official.
c Attempted Murder 25 minutes (+10 minutes per aggravated circumstance) When a person intentionally takes steps to kill someone or when they intentionally cause someone to be incapacitated (where they were revived by EMS). In order for this charge to stack it must be proven that the suspect took steps to attempts to kill each and every individual that the suspect is being accused of. (i.e. a person can not be charged with attempted murder for every single law enforcement officer on scene, it must be proven that the suspect attempted to take the life of the individual. Proof can be provided in the form of video footage, eye witness accounts (civilian or law enforcement officers), EMS testimony of injuries, footage or photos of EMS care, etc.) Aggravated circumstances: (i) The victim was an on-duty LEO or a government official.
2 VIOLENT CRIMES DEFINITION AGGRAVATING CIRCUMSTANCES
a Voluntary Manslaughter 20 minutes Intentionally causing the death of a person. Accompanied by additional circumstances that mitigate, but do not excuse the killing (such as when provoked, in terror, frightened, or desperate).
b Involuntary Manslaughter 15 minutes Unintentionally causing the death of a person without malice aforethought. Results when death occurs from an improper use of reasonable care or skill (recklessness or negligence) in commission of a lawful act or when death occurs while in the commission of an unlawful act not amounting to a felony.
c Vehicular Manslaughter 15 minutes Causing the death of a person with a vehicle due to the negligence or recklessness of the driver. This section will not apply where: (i) The person operating the motor vehicle reports to the police station to report the incident within a reasonable period of time; and (ii) the act was done without intent.
d Bank Robbery / Casino / Federal Reserve 35 minutes Stealing property from a bank / casino / federal reserve, including removing property from the vault.
e Robbery 20 minutes (+5 minutes for aggravated circumstance) The taking of money or goods in the possession of another, from his or her immediate person or immediate presence, by force or intimidation. In robbery, the victim feels there is an immediate threat of force or intimidation. Aggravated circumstances: (i) The victim was an on-duty LEO or a government official.
f Kidnapping 25 minutes (+10 minutes for aggravated circumstance) Unlawfully seizing and carrying away a person by force or fraud, or seizing and detaining a person against his or her will with an intent to carry that person away at a later time. In the case where multiple hostages are taken at once, only one charge will be presented. Aggravated circumstances: (i) The victim was an on-duty LEO or a government official. (ii) Using a deadly weapon in the commission of the offence.
g Arson 15 minutes Intentional destruction of property resulting in explosion or fire (bombing the prison door/bank vault, blowing up a vehicle, etc.)
